◈ Skill 1: Progressive Muscle Relaxation
▼
Helps reduce tension and calm your nervous system. Good when your body feels activated, heart is pounding, or you're having difficulty relaxing. The goal is not to force calm — it's to help your body notice the difference between tension and release.
1. Find a comfortable position. Take one slow breath in and one slow breath out.
2. Start with your feet. Gently tense the muscles for about 5 seconds, then release.
3. Move slowly: Feet → Legs → Stomach → Hands → Arms → Shoulders → Face
4. After each release, ask: "What feels different now?" · "Did my body soften, even slightly?" · "Where do I still feel tension?"

◈ Skill 2: Body Scan
▼
Helps you slow down and notice what is happening in your body without judging it. Helpful when anxiety, depression, irritability, or mood shifts make it hard to feel present.
1. Sit or lie down in a comfortable position. Take a slow breath.
2. Bring attention to your feet. Slowly move upward: Feet → Legs → Hips → Stomach → Chest → Hands → Arms → Shoulders → Neck → Jaw → Face → Head
3. At each area ask: "What do I feel here?" · "Is there tension, heaviness, warmth, tightness, or numbness?" · "Can I soften this area by 5%?"
4. You do not have to change anything. Just notice.

◈ Skill 3: Tomorrow Parking Lot

◈ Skill 4: Pause Before Reacting
▼
Emotional regulation doesn't mean you stop having strong emotions. It means you learn to notice what you're feeling, slow down your reaction, and choose a response that helps instead of creates more stress.
When I feel activated, I ask: What am I feeling? · What is my urge? · What do I need before I act? · What choice will help me later, not just right now?
